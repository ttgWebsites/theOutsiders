Characters

  • Ponyboy Curtis
  • Ponyboy is the novel's protagonist, he is a fourteen-year-old greaser.
  • The greasers are a clique or gang of teenage boys who wear matching white t-shirts, blue jeans, and slicked back hair.
  • Ponyboy lives with his two older brothers who are both greasers, as his parents died when he was younger.
  • Despite the greaser reputation, Ponyboy is an intelligent and good-natured person with desires to improve his life situation.
  • Darrel Curtis
  • Darrel is Ponyboy's oldest brother and is the provider for the family due to his parents deaths.
  • Darrel cares for his younger brothers and works hard to support them by working two jobs and tries to instill morals on them despite the greaser image.
  • Sodapop Curtis
  • Sodapop is the middle brother, he is much more eccentric than the other two Curtis boys.
  • Sodapop is the most outgoing boy and has intentions of marrying a greaser girl, Sandy.
  • Sandy
  • Sandy is Sodapop's girlfriend, however she does not appear in the novel, as she is just talked about.
  • She has moved to Florida after becoming pregnant with another man's baby.
  • Steve Randle
  • Steve is Sodapop's best friend as they have grown up together and currently work at the gas station together.
  • Steve is one of the tougher greasers, and is a tall and intimidating seventeen-year-old.
  • Ponyboy easily annoys him, as he does not believe he belongs with the other greasers.
  • Two-Bit Mathews
  • Two-Bit is quite the prankster and troublemaker.
  • He is not very tough, so his pranks usually end up coming back to bite the rest of the greasers.
  • One in particular, though not a prank, is when Two-Bit flirts with a Socs girl.
  • Socs are off limits, however Two-Bit does not care, it is this flirting that instigates a fight between the two groups.
  • Dallas 'Dally' Winston
  • Dallas is by far the toughest among the greasers, he grew up in New York, and is used to the street gangs there.
  • He is intimidating and is proud of his background, he is essentially the greasers muscle.
  • He sees the younger greaser, Johnny Cade, as his protégé and looks out for him.
  • Johnny Cade
  • Johnny is not the typical greaser, he is not very tough and seems to come off as fearful, however he has no other friends or a place to truly call home.
  • He finds comfort with the greasers, and the rest of the greasers see it as their job to look out for him.
  • Cherry Valance
  • Cherry is a Socs girl and is a cheerleader at Ponyboy's school.
  • She meets Ponyboy at the drive in theatre where he is with Dally.
  • She is not intimidated by the greasers, and in fact is more so infatuated with them.
  • Cherry and Ponyboy hit it off, and during the rest of the novel, it is clear that she sympathizes more with the greasers than her own kind.
  • Marcia
  • A Socs girl who is friends with Cherry, she meets Ponyboy and Two-Bit at the drive in.
  • She hits it off with Two-Bit, despite being in a relationship with a Socs boy, Randy.
  • Bob Shelton
  • Bob is a rich and entitled Socs, who clearly believes he is better than everyone else.
  • Before the novel begins he beats up Johnny Cade.
  • Bob makes sure to assert himself as someone not to mess with, he often wears three large rings to inflict the most damage possible in his fights.
  • Randy Adderson
  • Randy is Bob's best friend and the boyfriend of Marcia.
  • Randy realizes the futility of the rivalry between the greasers and Socs, and tries to draw a line in the sand before things get out of hand.
  • He sympathizes with Ponyboy and refuses to take fight in the brutal fight at the end of the novel.
  • Paul Holden
  • He is a Socs and tries to show the others just how tough he is.
  • In the fight he is the first to step up and call out Darry despite the two being friends in high school.
  • Jerry Wood
  • Jerry is a teacher, he is a good, well-intentioned man who holds off judgment based on stereotypes.
  • He accompanies Ponyboy to the hospital after Ponyboy rescues several young boys from a fire.
  • Tim Shepard
  • Tim is a greaser, however his group of greasers are ran a little bit differently then the group led by Dally.
  • His group is much tougher and more gang-oriented as opposed to the other greasers who seemingly just look out for one another.
  • Curly Shepard
  • Curly is Tim's younger brother, and is about the same age as Ponyboy.
  • He and Ponyboy differ greatly as Curly lacks morals, and it is encouraged by his older brother.
  • Curly is arrested for stealing liquor.
  • Mr. Syme
  • Mr. Syme is one of Ponyboy's teachers.
  • He recognizes Ponyboy's potential and is therefore disturbed by his lack of initiative.
  • It is one of his assignments that becomes the autobiography, The Outsiders. (though not really, just for the novel's sake.)
