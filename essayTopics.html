  • How does the Robert Frost poem "Nothing Gold Can Stay" play a role in the novel?
  • When Ponyboy and Johnny are hiding out after the murder of Bob they spend a lot of time reading.
  • Ponyboy reads a poem to Johnny by Robert Frost, the poem seems to put the boys situation into perspective as Frost compares the loss of innocence to the dying of flowers.
  • It suggests that everything beautiful has an inevitable end.
  • The boys recognize that their youth has come to an end. However with the help of the poem they come to realize that even so, there is more beauty left in life.

  • How do the characters Johnny and Dally compliment each other in the novel?
  • Johnny and Dally are very different characters among the greasers, however they are still very close.
  • Johnny is a young greaser, he still lives at home, however with often drunk and abusive parents.
  • Dally is the oldest of the greasers and he has already experienced a rough life.
  • It seems as if Dally sees a lot of himself in Johnny, and sees him as a younger brother.
  • He looks out for him and wants to protect him from many of the tougher aspects of being a greaser as he sees Johnny's potential and does not want him to end up like him.
  • Johnny on the other hand looks up to Dally, not for his way of life, but for his good intentions and the protection he provides him.
  • In the end when Johnny dies it is very clear just how much Dally cared for him.
  • Dally saw Johnny as somewhat of a way to make up for his past mistakes by helping him grow up.
  • When he dies Dally really has nothing left to live for, and therefore commits suicide by cops.
