Chapter Summaries

  • Chapter #1
  • The novel opens with Ponyboy Curtis, the narrator, telling of his experience walking home from a movie one day.
  • The movie was one of Paul Newman's films, and Ponyboy thinks he slightly envies his looks, however he also is pretty proud of his greaser look with his slicked back hair.
  • On the walk home Ponyboy acknowledges the serenity of going to films on his own, however he remarks that having company would be comforting for the walk home.
  • Ponyboy goes on to discuss the dangers of being alone in his city.
  • He is a greaser, or part of a clique of poorer and seemingly misguided individuals who dress in blue-jeans, white t-shirts, and slick back their hair.
  • As a greaser it is not safe being alone due to the attention it draws from another clique, the Socials, or Socs.
  • The Socs are made up of the richer teens from the West Side of town, they see themselves as far better than the greasers, however they are often more vile and seek out fights.
  • Ponyboy explains that when he was younger his mother and father died in a car crash, therefore leaving his eldest brother, Darrel 'Darry' in charge.
  • Darry looks out for Ponyboy, and expects him to stay out of the normal mischievous miscreant behavior of the greasers.
  • He sees Ponyboy's potential and longs for him to have a better life.
  • Ponyboy also has another brother, Sodapop, who is the middle child.
  • Sodapop is a very fun-loving character and is often up to mischief, however he is also good-intentioned.
  • Ponyboy switches back to discussing his walk home as he has now noticed that he is being trailed by a red Chevy Corvair, a typical Socs car.
  • Ponyboy hastens his walk as he remembers one of his fellow greasers, Johnny Cade, was beaten severely by a Socs group while he was walking alone one day.
  • Sure enough the Chevy pulls up to Ponyboy, and five Socs pile out surrounding him.
  • One pulls a blade and threatens him before they begin to beat him senseless as he cries for help.
  • Eventually he is rescued by his brothers and other Greasers who chase off the Socs.
  • Darry begins to scold Ponyboy for being out alone, however the other greasers recognize this is not the time for chastising.
  • After the commotion the boys make plans for the next night and Ponyboy decides that he will go to the movies again, this time with Johnny and Dally, an older greaser.
  • Dally discusses his former girlfriend, Sylvia, a greaser girl, and Ponyboy gets to thinking about what it would be like to date the likes of a Socs girl.
  • Later that evening Ponyboy settles in by reading Great Expectations, and Ponyboy relates himself to the protagonist Pip.
  • Shaken by the nights experience, Ponyboy settles into bed with his brother Sodapop and the two discuss Sodapop's girlfriend Sandy who he longs to marry one day.
  • Chapter #2
  • The boys follow through with the plans they made for the next evening and Ponyboy, Dally, and Johnny attend a double feature at the drive in.
  • They sit behind two Socs girls and Dally does his best to make them feel uncomfortable by talking dirty so that they will overhear him.
  • Sure enough one of the girls, Cherry Valence, turns around and tells him to grow up.
  • Dally is shocked, he decides to get up and buy some sodas, leaving Ponyboy in the company of the girls.
  • Ponyboy begins to talk to Cherry, and it does not appear awkward at all for a conversation between a Greaser and Socs.
  • They seem to hit it off and end up discussing Ponyboy's brother Sodapop and it appears Cherry has a soft spot for him.
  • Eventually Dally returns with cokes and offers one to Cherry, only for her to splash it in his face.
  • Dally is very embarrassed and tries to act tough, however Johnny, seemingly out of character, calls him out causing him to leave.
  • Johnny and Ponyboy sit down next to the girls and enjoy their company until they are interrupted by news from Two-Bit that Dally has gone and slashed the tires of Tim Shepard's car.
  • Tim is another greaser, and this is not acceptable among greasers.
  • It appears as if they plan to fight, and being that they are part of two separate greaser cliques, it is clear that Johnny and Ponyboy will have to come to Dally's defense.
  • Seeing as the fight has yet to happen, Ponyboy remains with the girls.
  • He and Cherry go off to buy popcorn and discuss the greasers and Socs.
  • He tells of the time Johnny was jumped by a gang of Socs, as well as his experience the night before.
  • She tries to defend the Socs by saying that not all are like that, and that they have issues just like the greasers.
  • Chapter #3
  • Following the movie the boys decide to walk Marcia and Cherry to Two-Bit's nearby house so they can give them a ride home.
  • On the walk they discuss Ponyboy's brothers, and Ponyboy is quite surprised at how well he and Cherry hit it off.
  • Cherry asks about Darry, and Ponyboy explains that he is frustrated with Ponyboy and probably feels life would be easier if he did not have to care for him.
  • Johnny and Two-Bit are surprised by this as they see the brothers relationship completely differently.
  • The conversation then switches to Socs and greasers again, and shockingly it appears that he and Cherry both have quite a bit in common despite the different cliques they are apart of.
  • However it soon switches to an argument as Ponyboy sees the Socs as privileged, selfish people who have nothing better to do than start trouble, while Cherry believes the greasers pity themselves too much and therefore pick fights because they lack self confidence.
  • The argument is futile and eventually it is given up.
  • As they continue the walk a blue Ford Mustang, the car of Bob Sheldon, Cherry’s boyfriend, passes them.
  • Randy Adderson who is Marcia’s boyfriend joins him.
  • The car pulls up to the greasers and the two Socs threaten a fight, however it is avoided when Marcia and Cherry agree to leave with them.
  • Cherry says something that surprises Ponyboy before leaving, she comments on how she could see herself falling for Dally despite his vulgar ways.
  • Eventually Ponyboy returns home well after 2 a.m. and Darry is furious as he is so late.
  • Darry scolds him and hits him causing Ponyboy to run from the house as he feels he isn't loved.
  • He find Johnny in the usual greaser hangout, and tells him they are running away.
  • Johnny, who lives with alcoholic parents, agrees without hesitation.
  • The two go on a walk and contemplate the real possibility of running away.
  • Chapter #4
  • While walking through a nearby park the boys notice the blue Mustang again, and realize that it has come back looking to pick a fight with the greasers for being out with their girlfriends.
  • This time there are five Socs who exit the car and begin harassing the boys.
  • Ponyboy defends himself by calling out the Socs for being nothing but phonies in expensive cars and clothes, he then spits on one of them.
  • This draws the ire of Bob who grabs Ponyboy and dunks his head in a fountain in attempt to drown him.
  • Ponyboy blacks out during the experience only to come to minutes later on the ground.
  • He notices the Socs have left, except one, Bob, who lies dead on the ground with a stab wound.
  • Johnny had stabbed him in attempt to save Ponyboy.
  • The boys realize the consequences of their actions and decide to consult Dally as to what to do.
  • They find him at one of his friends homes and Dally gives them fifty dollars and tells them to take a train to a nearby town, Windrixville, where there is an abandon church they can hide out in.
  • Several hours later they arrive at the church, exhausted.
  • Chapter #5
  • The next morning Ponyboy wakes up alone, he finds a note from Johnny.
  • Johnny has gone into town to buy supplies, he returns with food, cigarettes, a book (Gone With the Wind), and bleach to disguise their hair.
  • The boys spend the next week in hiding doing nothing else but reading Gone With the Wind to occupy their time.
  • The boys often try to relate the book and its characters to the greasers.
  • After hiding out for five days they are met by Dally who comes bearing a note from Sodapop.
  • In the note he learns that police have been searching for Bob's killer and that Sodapop told them that the murders fled to Texas from what he knows.
  • The boys and Dally go to Dairy Queen and discuss the events.
  • It is evident that a gang war is brewing between the Socs and greasers, and in a matter of days a fight is bound to occur.
  • Ponyboy also learns from Dally that due to Cherry's feelings that she was responsible for the death of Bob she has become a spy for the greasers.
  • Chapter #6
  • While the boys sit at Dairy Queen, Johnny comes out by saying that he would like to return home and confess.
  • He knows he did the right thing, and if he can get Ponyboy out of the mix, even better.
  • Dally is very fearful for this, as he sees Johnny as a brother and would not want to subject him to prison with the likes of hardened criminals.
  • Johnny however recognizes that his parents could care less and that confessing would make things easier.
  • Dally and the boys decide to drive home and figure it out from there, however on their way out of town they notice the church they had been staying at is on fire.
  • Thinking a cigarette butt must have caused it the boys decide to get out and check it out.
  • Much to their surprise they learn that this is not the cause, and that instead several school children were playing inside and the church set on fire.
  • The children were on a picnic nearby and had gone into the church to play.
  • Ponyboy, Johnny, and Dally rush inside as they hear the cries of the children.
  • They manage to escape with all the children through a window before the blaze causes the church to collapse.
  • Ponyboy realizes that Johnny has not escaped, and goes back in to save him.
  • He however is knocked unconscious and wakes up in an ambulance.
  • He learns from a schoolteacher, Jerry Wood, who was chaperoning the children's field trip that he is lucky to have survived.
  • Dally was burnt, however should be fine, Johnny on the other hand has been severely injured and they are unsure of his fate.
  • Jerry marvels at the boys lack of fear in the dangerous situation and calls them heroes, however Ponyboy claims that they are nothing more than delinquents.
  • While at the hospital Jerry keeps Ponyboy company as he recovers and Ponyboy tells the story of Bob's death.
  • Jerry sees it as self-defense and does not believe the boys should be punished.
  • Eventually Darry and Sodapop join them at the hospital.
  • Much to Ponyboy's surprise Darry is very emotional, and Ponyboy realizes that his brother is only strict out of love.
  • The brothers embrace and Ponyboy genuinely believes things will go back to normal
  • Chapter #7
  • In the hospital room police and reporters arrive to question Ponyboy about the events of the fire.
  • Sodapop is in a very playful mood and seems to cheer up everyone with his joking ways.
  • The group that has assembled learns that Dally will fully recover; however Johnny on the other hand may not live, and if he does he is sure to be permanently handicapped.
  • Ponyboy leaves the hospital that day with his brothers and returns home.
  • The following morning while Ponyboy is making breakfast he is met by Steve Randle, Sodapop's friend, and Two-Bit.
  • The two greasers show Ponyboy newspapers that talk of the heroics of Johnny, Ponyboy, and Dally.
  • The papers also touch on Bob's death and say that Johnny will be charged with manslaughter, and that Ponyboy will go to court as well.
  • It is possible that Ponyboy will be sent somewhere else to live, and the thought greatly frightens him.
  • Ponyboy thinks back to a reoccurring nightmare he has had, and though he never remembers the dream, it startles him greatly and often.
  • Following breakfast Darry and Sodapop go to work and leave Ponyboy with the other greaser boys.
  • Ponyboy learns that recently, Sandy, Sodapop's girlfriend, left to Florida as she is pregnant, and her parents forbid her from marrying Sodapop.
  • Ponyboy and Two-Bit head down to a nearby restaurant for cokes and notice the blue Socs Mustang has arrived.
  • Ponyboy is furious and despises the Socs for what has become of him.
  • He is shocked to see one of the Socs, Randy exiting the car and heading towards him.
  • He knows that the Socs and Greasers can't fight until the scheduled gang rumble, however he is fearful nonetheless.
  • Randy surprises Ponyboy by showing compassion and discussing the events at the church.
  • Randy expresses shock that a greaser would show compassion like that, and the two come to realize that they are more alike then they believed.
  • Randy finishes by expressing regret over the death of Bob, and that he does not blame the greasers as Bob's temper got the best of him.
  • The two discuss the rumble and Randy claims he wants no part of it.
  • The conversation ends and it appears as if both boys have matured greatly due to the discussion.
  • Chapter #8
  • That afternoon Ponyboy and Two-Bit visit Johnny at the hospital.
  • Johnny is very weak, but he shows much love towards his two friends.
  • He asks Ponyboy to read to him and he does before they are interrupted by Johnny's mother.
  • Johnny refuses to see her as it is obvious she does not care much for him.
  • On the way out of the hospital she tries to blame Two-Bit and Ponyboy for her sons accident, however Two-Bit insults her and the boys leave to see Dally in a nearby room.
  • Dally is very kind to Ponyboy for the first time, and Ponyboy feels compassion for him.
  • The boys discuss the rumble and Dally asks for Two-Bit's switch blade, which he gladly gives up.
  • On the way home the boys come across Cherry and discuss the fight, she explains that the Socs have agreed to no weapons, however this seems unlikely.
  • Ponyboy encourages her to visit Johnny however she refuses as she is still upset about Bob, and defends Bob as being a good guy, and only violent when drunk.
  • Ponyboy is upset and calls her a traitor, however he calms and settles things before leaving.
  • He asks her if she can see the sunset from the West Side, which she can, and reminds her that he can see it too before heading off.
  • Chapter #9
  • At home Ponyboy nervously awaits the upcoming rumble, he takes several aspirin in hopes of calming his nerves, however this is to no avail.
  • The other greasers are in much higher spirits as they seem to be excited for the impending brawl.
  • The Dally's greaser group meets up with the group led by Tim Shepard and they prepare for the planned fight.
  • Eventually the Socs arrive, there are twenty-two all together, up against twenty greasers.
  • The fight begins with Darry stepping forward and being challenged by a former football friend, Paul Holden.
  • Darry steps in as well and the fight fully breaks out.
  • Eventually it ends as a victory for the greasers and Ponyboy and Dally take off as quick as possible to go check on Johnny.
  • Initially they are stopped by a policeman, however Ponyboy is able to convince him he is injured, allowing for an escort to the hospital.
  • Once at the hospital the two boys make their way to Johnny's room, only to find him in worse shape than he was before.
  • Johnny speaks very softly and complains that the Socs and greaser fight is pointless.
  • He then whispers "stay gold" to Ponyboy before slipping out of consciousness and into death.
  • Dally is extremely shaken, he leaves the room in tears.
  • Chapter #10
  • Ponyboy leaves the hospital as well, he walks around aimlessly in a daze before he is picked up by a passing motorist.
  • Ponyboy is brought home where he tells the other greasers of Johnny's death and Dally's disappearance.
  • Eventually the greasers hear from Dally who explains that he has just robbed a grocery store and is being chased by the police.
  • The greasers rush outside only to see Dally pull an unloaded gun on the cops, causing them to shoot him.
  • It is clear to Ponyboy that Dally intended this outcome.
  • Ponyboy collapses after seeing Dally's death, he awakes three days later and learns that he had a severe concussion in the brawl with the Socs.
  • Chapter #11
  • Ponyboy spends the next week in bed recovering; one afternoon he decides to look at Sodapop's old yearbook.
  • He discovers a picture of Bob, the Socs that died.
  • In the picture Bob is grinning, and his look appears to Ponyboy to resemble Sodapop.
  • Ponyboy thinks about Bob's family and how they must hate him and the greasers.
  • He laments that Bob was rather cocky and ill-tempered, however his traits were very human.
  • Randy, Bob's friend arrives to speak with Ponyboy about the upcoming trial.
  • Ponyboy is still rather delirious and claims to Randy that he was the one that killed Bob, and that Johnny is still alive.
  • Randy is worried about his image in the situation, and is somewhat aggressive towards Ponyboy, to the point that Darry asks him to leave.
  • Chapter #12
  • Ponyboy attends the hearing, however he is very quiet in part to his concussion.
  • After testimony the judge clears him of all charges and allows him to return home with his brothers.
  • Back at home it is clear that Ponyboy is not the same, his attitude changes, his grades suffer, and he resumes his regular fights with Darry.
  • He is nearly failing out of school, and his English teacher offers him a way to pull his grade up by writing an exceptional autobiographical essay.
  • The following day Ponyboy hangs out with Steve and Two-Bit at lunch.
  • They walk down to the grocery store for candy and soda when they are accosted by a group of Socs.
  • Surprisingly Ponyboy is very hostile towards the Socs, and threatens them.
  • The two greasers with him see this as very unusual for him.
  • However when Ponyboy relaxes after the encounter, he shows his true colors by picking up the broken glass from a bottle he used to threaten the Socs.
  • He claims he does not want anyone to get a flat tire.
  • That evening Darry and Ponyboy get into another loud argument causing Sodapop to flee the house.
  • Ponyboy learns from Darry that Sodapop is suffering due to his former girlfriend Sandy.
  • Her baby is not Sodapop's and she is ignoring him.
  • The two stop their fighting and go in search of Sodapop.
  • Sodapop is very upset and Darry and Ponyboy comfort him, vowing to quit fighting.
  • The boys return home and back inside Ponyboy discovers his copy of Gone With the Wind, inside the book is a note from Johnny.
  • The note asks Ponyboy to remain golden, and Johnny explains that saving the children's lives was far more important than his life.
  • In that moment Ponyboy knows what he will write about for his autobiographical essay.
  • He begins The Outsiders with the opening lines seen earlier in the novel, he hopes to show other hoodlums and hopeless kids that there is still beauty in the world.
